*How does the word Health & Wellness related to each other?

Health: A state of being that includes physical, mental & social well-being
Wellness: The act of living in a healthy and fulfilling way, which involves making choices and taking actions to improve health. In understanding the difference between health and wellness, in short, health is a state of being, whereas wellness is the state of living a healthy lifestyle.Health refers to physical, mental, and social well-being; wellness aims to enhance well-being.


Mindfulness and Meditation Techniques

Mindfulness and meditation are practices that can enhance well-being, reduce stress, and promote a deeper understanding of the self. Here's an overview of both concepts and various techniques you can explore:

Key principals of mindfulness~
  1. *Awareness*: Being conscious of what you are doing and feeling.
  2. *Non-judgment*: Accepting your thoughts and feelings without labeling them as good or bad.
  3. *Focus on the Present*: Engaging with the here and now rather than dwelling on the past or worrying about the future.

Mindfulness Techniques~

  1. Mindful Breathing: Focus on the natural rhythm of your breath. Notice the sensations of inhaling and exhaling. If your mind wanders, gently bring your attention back to your breath.
  2. Body Scan: Lie down or sit comfortably and systematically focus on different parts of your body. Notice any sensations, tensions, or feelings without trying to change them.
  3. Mindful Eating: Eat slowly and pay attention to the taste, texture, and smell of your food. Notice how it feels to chew and swallow, and be aware of your hunger and fullness cues.
  4. Walking Meditation: Take a slow, deliberate walk while paying attention to the sensations of walking—the feeling of your feet on the ground, the movement of your legs, and the rhythm of your breath.
  5. Mindful Observation: Choose something in your environment (like a tree, a flower, or a piece of art) and observe it closely. Notice colors, shapes, textures, and details, allowing yourself to be fully present with that object.

Meditation Techniques~

  1. Guided Meditation: Follow along with a recorded meditation or a live instructor who provides guidance. This can include visualizations or focus on specific themes.
  2. Loving-Kindness Meditation (Metta): Focus on cultivating feelings of love and compassion towards yourself and others. Start by repeating phrases like “May I be happy, may I be healthy” and gradually extend these wishes to others.
  3. Zen Meditation (Zazen): Sit in a specific posture and focus on your breath, allowing thoughts to come and go without engagement. This can include counting breaths or simply being aware of the breath.
  4. Transcendental Meditation (TM): This involves silently repeating a mantra for about 20 minutes, twice a day, to settle the mind and achieve a relaxed state of awareness.
  5. Visualization: Picture a serene scene in your mind, such as a beach or forest. Engage all your senses to enhance this mental imagery, focusing on how it feels, sounds, and smells.
